Market hedges more than the model

The model puts Spain 17 pp higher than the market — 75% vs 58%.

The model assigns Spain a 75% win probability while sportsbooks price them at 58%—a 17-percentage-point gap favoring the model's confidence in a Spanish victory. This gap could reflect recent form or injury news that has reached the market but not the model's last training update; lineups or squad availability changes announced closer to the match would naturally dampen bookmaker conviction even if underlying Elo ratings remain stable. Alternatively, the market may be pricing in late-breaking information about either team's condition or readiness. The dashboard surfaces this divergence as a signal worth monitoring: it shows real money is hedging against the model's high Spain win probability, but both sources agree Spain is the clear favorite.

The model favors Spain heavily at 75%, leaving Belgium just 4% to win outright. That 71-percentage-point gap reflects a stark asymmetry: the model sees Spain as commanding favorites, while Belgium's upset probability sits near zero by this measure.

A decisive contest favoring one side appears most likely under the model, but a stalemate remains plausible at roughly one-in-five odds.
