Title odds — who wins the World Cup?

Each contender's chance of lifting the trophy, from three sources side by side: the statistical model, sportsbook outright odds (vig stripped), and Polymarket prices. Ranked by the model. Bars share one scale, so shorter bars mean a lower title probability.

Read this first

The model is structurally more top-heavy than the markets — its two favourites hold roughly 50% of the title probability between them versus about 34% across the markets. A large model-over-market gap at the top is expected behaviour of a pure Elo + Dixon-Coles model with no injury, form, or squad data, not a sign the model knows something the market doesn't. Treat the figures near the top as directional, not precise. See the methodology for why.
